Play Mario Party 6, natively, from your own disc

This is the companion site for the mp6-native-port project: a native Windows and Android port of the Mario Party 6 decompilation. It doesn't emulate anything -- the game's own decompiled source runs directly on your machine. This page turns your own legally-owned disc plus the project's engine build into a folder (or APK) you can run, entirely in your browser.

What this actually does

Everything happens in your browser tab, locally. There is no server component and no upload: your disc image is read with the File API directly off your own disk, parsed, filtered down to the ~400MB subset of files the port actually needs, and written straight to a folder you choose (or, on browsers without that capability, streamed into a single ZIP download) alongside the project's own prebuilt engine. Close the tab and nothing of yours is left anywhere but your own disk.

Legal / policy

This project ships no game data, ever, in any form -- not in this repository, not in a Release, not anywhere this site touches. What the project's GitHub Releases page carries is a content-free engine build: on Windows, that's mp6native.exe plus a handful of DLLs and a small resource folder (fonts and UI stylesheets) -- no game code, no assets. On Android, it's a clean APK of roughly 30MB, same story. Neither contains one byte of Mario Party 6.

To actually play, you combine that engine with game content extracted from your own, legally owned Mario Party 6 (USA) disc -- either through this site's packager (Windows) or the app's own on-device import (Android). That extraction happens locally, using this project's decompiled understanding of the disc's file layout; your disc image or its bytes never leave your machine, and this site cannot see them.

The install this produces is for your own personal use. Please don't rehost or redistribute the folder, ZIP, or APK-imported content this tool produces -- it contains copyrighted game assets decoded from your disc, and redistributing it would be redistributing Nintendo/Hudson's copyrighted work, not this project's. Share the engine (that part's fine, it's already public in Releases) and point people at their own disc for the rest.

This is an unofficial fan project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Nintendo, Hudson Soft, or any of their successors or affiliates in any way.
